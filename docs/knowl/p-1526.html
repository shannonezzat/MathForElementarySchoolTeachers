We'll cross off \(1\) since we know that it doesn't have exactly two factors; its only factor is itself. So now we know that the next largest number, \(2\text{,}\) must be prime since it has \(1\) and itself as a factor, and there are no other numbers smaller than \(2\text{.}\) So we circle \(2\) and cross off all numbers that have \(2\) as a factor.
