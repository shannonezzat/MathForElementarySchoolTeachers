A quick reminder that in base twelve, we ony regroup when we have twleve of one place. We represent ten of a place by the symbol \(A\) and eleven of a place by the symbol \(B\text{.}\) Also, note that in this section we will assume all calculations are in base twelve, even if we do not note the base for each number; that is, for example, we'll write \(3A \times 15\) to mean \(3A_{twelve} \times 15_{twelve}\text{.}\)
