Specifically, a number \(a\) is said to be divisible by another number \(b\) if there exists a number \(c\) such that \(a = bc\text{.}\) This is often expressed in various ways, such as "\(b\) is a factor of \(a\)", "\(b\) divides \(a\)", "\(a\) is a multiple of \(b\)", or "\(a\) is divisible by \(b\)".
