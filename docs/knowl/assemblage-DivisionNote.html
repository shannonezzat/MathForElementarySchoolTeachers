Notes for Additional Understanding.

If we have a remainder that isn't zero, things get a little more complicated. For example, look at the division equation \(64 \div 40 = 1R24\text{,}\) but since our remainder is quite large (notably it's bigger than our quotient) the related problem \(64 \div 1\) obviously does not equal \(24\text{.}\)

As a challenge problem, see if you can come up with a general result when \(a \div b = qRr\) relating to \(a \div q\text{.}\)
