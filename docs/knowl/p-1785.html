We know how many total factors there are, and we know how many prime factors there are. Let \(x\) be the number of composite factors of \(n\text{.}\) Remembering that factors are either composite, prime, or the number \(1\text{,}\) we can say that \(4+1+x=144\) and thus \(x=139\text{.}\)
