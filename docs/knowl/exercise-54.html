Checkpoint 6.3.3.

We found a list of prime numbers less than 100. Let's see if we notice some patterns about primes by doing the same for numbers less than, say, 500.

  1. Make a table of all numbers up to 500. You can probably search one online and print it off if that makes it easier.

  2. Perform the sieve as above. It will take a bit longer, but it will be the exact same process.

  3. What can you say about the amount of smaller prime numbers compared to the number of larger (at least closer to 500) prime numbers? Why do you think this is the case?

  4. On the same chart, highlight all multiples of 6. What can you say about how prime numbers relate to multiples of 6? Formulate your conjectures in terms of remainders.

  5. Try and prove the conjecture you made in the previous part. Hint.
