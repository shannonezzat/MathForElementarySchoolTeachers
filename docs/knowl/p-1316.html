  1. Divide 6 by 13. Since 6 is less than 13, we add a decimal point and a zero to the right of 6, making it 60.

  2. Now, divide 60 by 13. The result is 4 with a remainder of 8.

  3. Add another zero to the right of the remainder, making it 80.

  4. Divide 80 by 13. The result is 6 with a remainder of 2.

  5. Repeat this process. Each time, we add a zero to the right of the remainder and divide by 13.

  6. Eventually, we notice that the remainders start to repeat, indicating a repeating decimal.
