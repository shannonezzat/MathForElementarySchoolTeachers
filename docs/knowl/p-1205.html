You have most likely learned in school that to divide to fractions, you multiply the first fraction by the reciprocal of the second fraction; for example \(\frac{2}{3} \div {4}{5} = \frac{2}{3} \times {5}{4}.\) Of course this is correct, but as we are in the business of understanding why these ideas are correct, let's look more closely at why this idea is true.
