Example 4.1.7.

Consider the fractions $\displaystyle \frac{2}{3}$ and $\displaystyle \frac{4}{6}$. These fractions are equivalent because $2 \times 6 = 3 \times 4$. Equivalently, since we can multiply each of \(2\) and \(3\) by \(2\) to get \(4\) and \(6\) (or divide \(4\) and \(6\) by \(2\text{,}\) conversely), you can see that these are equivalent fractions.
