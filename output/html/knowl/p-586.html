The division \(a \div b\) can be viewed in another way as well; we can ask if we wish to partition \(a\) objects into \(b\) groups, how many objects are in each group? This is called the partitive model of division (the word "partitive" being related to the idea of a partition). Again, we look at \(43 \div 4\) using this model
