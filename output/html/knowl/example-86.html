Example 4.2.6. Converting a Mixed Fraction to an Improper Fraction.

Let's convert the mixed fraction \(3\left(\frac{2}{5}\right)\) to an improper fraction. First, we convert the whole number part 3 to the fraction \(\frac{3}{1}\text{.}\)

Now, we need to find equivalent fractions for \(\frac{3}{1}\) and \(\frac{2}{5}\) with a common denominator. In this case, the least common denominator is 5. We multiply the numerator and denominator of \(\frac{3}{1}\) by 5 to get the equivalent fraction \(\frac{15}{5}\text{.}\)

Now, we can add the numerators of the equivalent fractions: \(15 + 2 = 17\text{.}\) Keeping the common denominator, we get the improper fraction \(\frac{17}{5}\text{.}\) So, \(3\left(\frac{2}{5}\right) = \frac{17}{5}\text{.}\)
