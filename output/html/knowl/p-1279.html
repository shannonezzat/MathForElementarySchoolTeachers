An interesting aspect of decimal fractions is the similarity they share with whole numbers. We know that multiplying a whole number by 10 raises its place value by 1, such as multiplying 10 by 10k gives us 10k+1. In a similar manner, multiplying a decimal fraction by 10 also shifts its place value to the left. For example, multiplying 1/10k by 10 gives us 1/10k-1. This relationship holds true as we move along the decimal places and will allow us to perform arithmetic operations similarly to arithmetic with whole numbers.
