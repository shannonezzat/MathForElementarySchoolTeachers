Let's arrange our equation so that we have no roots or denominators: this means multiplying both sides by \(b\) and then squaring both sides of the equation:

\begin{equation*} \sqrt{n}=\frac{a}{b} \rightarrow \sqrt{n}b=a \rightarrow nb^2=a^2. \end{equation*}

In our example:

\begin{equation*} \sqrt{60}=\frac{a}{b} \rightarrow \sqrt{60}b=a \rightarrow 60b^2=a^2. \end{equation*}
