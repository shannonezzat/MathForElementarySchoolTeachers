Divide \(a\) by \(b\) to get a quotient \(q\) and a remainder \(r\text{,}\) where \(0 \leq r < b\text{.}\) Then, the GCD of \(a\) and \(b\) is the same as the GCD of \(b\) and \(r\text{.}\) Continue this process, replacing \(a\) with \(b\) and \(b\) with \(r\text{,}\) until \(r\) is zero. The GCD is the non-zero remainder from the previous step.
