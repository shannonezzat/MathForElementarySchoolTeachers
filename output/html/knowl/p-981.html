By the property of multiplicative identity, we know that \(1 \times 5 = 5\) and we know that \(2 \times 5 = 5 + 5 = A\text{,}\) since we have ten ones (not enough to regoup). We can determine \(3 \times 5\) by adding \(5\) to \(2 \times 5\text{.}\) Thus we have \(3 \times 5 = 2 \times 5 +5 = A+5 = 13\text{.}\) We can input these into our times table in both the 5 row and 5 column since multiplication is commutative: \(5 \times n = n \times 5\) for any number \(n\text{.}\)
