\begin{equation*} \frac{1500 - 2000}{2000} \times 100 = -25\% \end{equation*}

Therefore, the percent change is -25%. This negative value indicates a decrease in the population. This example demonstrates how percent change can effectively communicate both increases and decreases in values, providing a clear picture of how quantities evolve over time.
