Proof

Assume that the if-part is true; that is \(a-b=c\text{.}\) By the definition of subtraction, we know that this means that \(a=b+c\text{.}\) Now, by using commutativity of addition we can write this as \(a = c+b\text{,}\) which, by the definition of subtraction, is the same as the subtraction problem \(a-c=b\text{.}\)
