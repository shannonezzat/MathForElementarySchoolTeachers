Since elements in an intersection need to satisfy *either* being a memebr of A or B, we have that \(A \cup C = \{ \text{ all triangles and all polygons of at most 5 sides.} \}\text{.}\) However, triangles are polygons of at most 5 sides (they have three), we have that \(A \subseteq C\) and thus we can express \(A \cup C = \{ \text{ all polygons of at most 5 sides.} \}\) and note \(A \cup C = C\text{.}\)
