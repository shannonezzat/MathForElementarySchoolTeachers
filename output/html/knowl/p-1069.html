Consider the division problem \(a \div b\) where \(a,b \in mathbb{N}.\) The idea behind long division uses the partitive mode of division along with the idea of place value. Starting with the largest place value of the dividend \(a\text{,}\) we group as many of those place into \(b\) groups. Any of that place value remaining we ungroup into the next largest place value. A quick note: remember from the Quotient Remainder Theorem that remainders are always between \(0\) and \(b-1\) (inclusive) so we know each remainder in our calculation, which we will call a \(place remainder\text{,}\) must be within this range.
