Let \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) where \(n\) has a prime factorization of \(n=p_1^{e_1}p_2^{e_2}\ldots p_k^{e_k}\) for some primes \(p_1, \ldots, p_k\) and non-negative exponents \(e_1, \ldots, e_k\text{.}\) Then \(n\) has \((e_1+1) \times (e_2 +1) \times \ldots \times (e_k+1)\) factors in total.
