Example 3.5.10.

Let's say we wanted to do the division problem \(21 \div 5.\) We can view this as counting the number of times we can subtract 5 from 21 until we're unable to anymore (without going in to negative numbers). We know

\begin{equation*} 21-5=16 16-5=11 11-5=6 6-5=1 \end{equation*}

and since 1 is less than 5 we cannot subtract 5 from 1 using our definition of subtraction. We were able to subtract 5 four times from 21, so we say that \(21 \div 5 = 4 \ R \ 1\) since there is 1 remaning at the end of this repeated subtraction. Let's look at this in general:
