A mixed number is a number that is represented as the sum of a whole number and a proper fraction. It is usually written in the form \(a\left(\frac{b}{c}\right)\) which really means \(a + \frac{b}{c}\) but written without the addition sign between the whole number and fraction, where \(a\) is a whole number, and \(\frac{b}{c}\) is a proper fraction, meaning \(b < c\text{.}\)
