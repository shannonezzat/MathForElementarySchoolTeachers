Another way to do this, but which might end up with fractions with larger integers making up the numerators and denominators, is to multiply the numerator and denominator of a fraction by the denominator of the other, and vice versa. Thus, if we wanted \(\frac{a}{b}\) and \(\frac{c}{d}\) to have a common denominator, we multiply the top and bottom of the first fraction by \(d\) and the second fraction by \(b\) to get \(\frac{ad}{bd}\) and \(\frac{bc}{bd}\text{.}\) Now both quantities are fractions that have the same denominator.
