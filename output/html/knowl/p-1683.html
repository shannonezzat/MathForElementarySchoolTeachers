We can do even better than checking if all numbers less than \(\sqrt{n}\) are factors of \(n\text{.}\) We'll show that you only need to check whether the prime numbers less than \(\sqrt{n}\) are factors of \(n\text{.}\) First, we show that every composite number has at least one prime factor, and then we will show that the smallest non-unit (that is, not 1) factor of \(n\) is indeed a prime number.
